Here's what I'm thinking for the core prompt structure:
- Role & Purpose: You're [Name], a 24-year-old supportive best friend helping your friend get ready for Navy basic training in November by suggesting healthier food swaps and creating beginner-friendly workouts.
- What You Know About the User: She's 24, new to working out, has low stamina, loves sugar, and needs to build up to more push-ups, sit-ups, and running 1.5 miles.

Test Prompt: Give me a monthly workout schedule focusing on push ups, sit ups, running, and full body

Gymbot: Here's a beginner-friendly monthly workout schedule that progressively builds strength and endurance:

Week 1: Building the Foundation
- Monday: 3 sets of knee push-ups (as many as possible), 3 sets of crunches (10 reps), 10-minute walk
- Tuesday: Rest or light stretching
- Wednesday: 3 sets of wall push-ups (10 reps), 3 sets of sit-ups (8 reps), 15-minute walk
- Thursday: Full body - 2 sets of bodyweight squats (10 reps), 2 sets of lunges (8 each leg), 2 sets of plank holds (15 seconds)
- Friday: Rest
- Saturday: 3 sets of knee push-ups (as many as possible), 3 sets of sit-ups (10 reps), 0.25 mile jog + walk intervals
- Sunday: Active recovery - light yoga or stretching

Week 2: Slight Increase
- Monday: 3 sets of knee push-ups (add 2 more reps than last week), 3 sets of sit-ups (12 reps), 12-minute jog/walk
- Tuesday: Rest or stretching
- Wednesday: 4 sets of incline push-ups (using a bench/chair, 8 reps), 3 sets of bicycle crunches (12 reps), 18-minute walk/jog
- Thursday: Full body - 3 sets of squats (12 reps), 2 sets of step-ups (10 each leg), 2 sets of plank holds (20 seconds)
- Friday: Rest
- Saturday: 3 sets of knee push-ups, 3 sets of sit-ups (12 reps), 0.5 mile jog with walk breaks
- Sunday: Active recovery

Week 3: Building Endurance
- Monday: 4 sets of incline push-ups (10 reps), 4 sets of sit-ups (15 reps), 15-minute jog/walk
- Tuesday: Rest or light stretching
- Wednesday: 3 sets of regular push-ups (as many as possible) + knee push-ups to finish, 3 sets of Russian twists (15 reps), 20-minute jog/walk
- Thursday: Full body - 3 sets of jump squats (8 reps), 3 sets of lunges (12 each leg), 3 sets of plank holds (25 seconds), 2 sets of mountain climbers (10 reps)
- Friday: Rest
- Saturday: 4 sets of push-up variations (mix regular and knee), 4 sets of sit-ups (15 reps), 0.75 mile jog with minimal walking
- Sunday: Active recovery - stretching or gentle yoga

Week 4: Pushing Further
- Monday: 4 sets of regular push-ups (aim for more than Week 3), 4 sets of sit-ups (20 reps), 18-minute continuous jog/walk
- Tuesday: Rest or stretching
- Wednesday: 5 sets of push-ups (regular form, as many as possible), 4 sets of V-ups or full sit-ups (12 reps), 25-minute jog/walk
- Thursday: Full body - 3 sets of burpees (6 reps), 3 sets of squats (15 reps), 3 sets of plank to downward dog (10 reps), 2 sets of jumping jacks (20 reps)
- Friday: Rest
- Saturday: Max push-ups test (see how many you can do!), max sit-ups test (1 minute), 1 mile run attempt (jog as much as possible)
- Sunday: Full rest day - you earned it!

Key Tips:

- Warm up for 5 minutes before each workout (jumping jacks, arm circles, leg swings)
- Cool down and stretch after every session
- If something feels too hard, scale back—it's better to do modified versions correctly than struggle with bad form
- Hydrate throughout the day, not just during workouts
- Track your progress each week so you can see improvement!
